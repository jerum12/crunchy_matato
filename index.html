About Us

Malunggay and potato fries are two food items that are distinct in terms of their origins and nutritional content. Malunggay, also known as moringa, is a plant that is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South Asia. It is widely recognized for its high nutritional value and medicinal properties, and its leaves are used in various cuisines across the world. Malunggay leaves are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, and they are known to have anti-inflammatory and anti-cancer properties. Potatoes, on the other hand, are a root vegetable that are native to the Andes region of South America. They are a staple food in many parts of the world and are widely used in a variety of dishes, including fries. While potatoes are not as nutrient-dense as malunggay, they are a good source of complex carbohydrates, potassium, and vitamin C. When combined, malunggay and potato fries create a unique and delicious snack that is both tasty and nutritious. The addition of malunggay to the fries provides an extra boost of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, while also adding a unique flavor profile to the dish. Whether enjoyed as a snack or as a side dish, malunggay and potato fries offer a healthy and flavorful alternative to traditional fast food options.
